## Brand Colors

### Primary Colors
Used most frequently across platforms, marketing materials, and communications.

| Name | Hex | RGB | CMYK | Role |
|------|-----|-----|------|------|
| Cream Blush | `#F8EEE5` | 248, 238, 229 | 2%, 5%, 8%, 0% | Primary |
| Light Pink | `#FFB6BA` | 251, 182, 186 | 0%, 35%, 15%, 0% | Primary |

### Secondary Colors
Used to support and accent the primary palette. Green can function as primary or secondary depending on the scenario.

| Name | Hex | RGB | CMYK | Role |
|------|-----|-----|------|------|
| Medium Pink | `#FF879E` | 249, 135, 158 | 0%, 59%, 17%, 0% | Secondary |
| Hot Pink | `#FA5185` | 248, 82, 133 | 0%, 83%, 21%, 0% | Secondary |
| Warm Brown | — | 138, 90, 62 | 36%, 63%, 78%, 24% | Secondary |
| Deep Green | `#015A42` | 18, 90, 66 | 87%, 40%, 78%, 35% | Primary or Secondary |

### Color Usage Notes
- Lighter pinks and green = primary brand colors
- Brown, hot pink, and green = secondary colors
- Not all color combos will work — refer to the PDF's "Best Color Variations" page for approved combinations
- Avoid combinations with poor legibility (see "Worst Color Variations" in PDF)
- Accessibility is critical across all platforms (socials, website, etc.)

## Typography

### Title & Headings — Editor's Note
- **Style:** Script / premium font
- **Sizes:** H1 at 60px · H2 at 35–40px
- **Purchase:** Jen Wagnor's website (premium)
- **Characters:** A–Z (upper & lower), 0–9, special characters

### Subheading — Open Sans
- **Style:** All caps
- **Sizes:** 13–18px depending on platform
- **Purchase:** Creative Market (premium)

### Paragraph — Open Sans
- **Style:** Regular
- **Sizes:** 14–17px depending on platform
- **Available:** Free Google Font

## Logo System

### Mirella Manelli Logos

**Primary Logo**
- Script-style handwritten font — showcases the colorful, fun, approachable, yet authoritative side of the brand
- The two **M's** are pivotal to all logo variations and drive brand recognition
- Used most frequently across all branding

**Secondary Logo**
- Clean, straight-forward, and professional sans-serif
- Use when the primary logo is too long for a space, or doesn't feel right for a specific situation
- Great for website use

**Submarks**
- Simple, small, easily recognizable
- Use in condensed spaces where primary/secondary logos don't fit
- Best for: social media profile pictures, packaging, marketing material accents

**Favicon**
- For web browser representation

---

### Hair B&B Logos

**Relationship to Mirella Manelli Brand**
- Designed as a **sister logo** — cohesive with Mirella Manelli, but able to stand alone as its own brand
- The two **B's** mirror the M's in the Mirella Manelli logos — pivotal to all Hair B&B logo variations

**Primary Logo**
- Sans-serif text style
- Can be used interchangeably for "university" and "podcast"
- 'Hair B&B' text can be used without subtext when needed
- Used most frequently in Hair B&B branding

**Secondary Logo**
- Use when the primary is too long for a space
- Text can be used interchangeably for 'university' and 'podcast'

**Submarks & Favicon**
- Same usage rules as Mirella Manelli submarks
